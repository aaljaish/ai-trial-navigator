AI Trial Navigator

The AI Trial Navigator is a modular, task‑oriented application that guides trialists from formulating a clinical question to producing a protocol‑ready operational plan. By breaking down the complex process of pragmatic trials into digestible sections, the Navigator helps teams maintain scientific rigour, equity and governance, operational feasibility and sustainable resourcing. Built‑in AI support identifies gaps, offers refinements and flags potential risks—empowering teams to iterate and improve.

Core Question

Define the clinical question (PICO‑T)

Begin by clearly defining the question using the PICO framework—Population, Intervention, Comparator and Outcome—to ensure alignment across all aspects of the trial. Specify inclusion/exclusion criteria and primary/secondary outcomes alongside measurement windows, and set the follow‑up cadence.

Eligibility & Setting
  • Describe the clinical and community settings where participants will be recruited and treated.
  • Outline screening processes and leverage electronic health records or registries for efficient identification.

Design & Methods

Trial Design & Randomization

Choose the appropriate design—parallel, factorial or cluster—and plan allocation concealment and blinding to eliminate bias. Define unit of randomization, stratification and sequence.

Sample Size & Power

Calculate the sample size needed to detect meaningful effects while balancing resources and feasibility. Document assumptions (effect size, variability) and plan interim analyses if needed.

Estimand & Intercurrent Events

Define the estimand—the quantity to be estimated—and describe how intercurrent events (e.g., protocol deviations) will be handled in analysis.

Operationalization

Site Readiness & Logistics
  • Assess capacity, equipment and workflow for each participating site.
  • Provide training and competency materials with refreshers as needed.
  • Map EHR triggers, order sets and steps for intervention delivery.
Recruitment, Retention & Risk Management

Design recruitment and retention strategies that minimize burden and maximize engagement. Identify key risks (e.g., recruitment shortfalls, data issues) and mitigation plans. Develop a monitoring plan focusing on critical data and processes.

Data & IT

Data Sources & Capture
  • Identify data sources (EHR, claims, registries) and plan secure linkage via unique identifiers.
  • Configure electronic case report forms (eCRFs) and centralized quality checks.
Missing Data & Security
  • Develop strategies to prevent and handle missing data (e.g., imputation methods).
  • Establish security, privacy and retention policies; create a data dictionary.

Governance & Ethics

Ethics & Regulatory Approvals
  • Obtain ethical approval from a central or site-specific research board.
  • Define Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) reporting cadence and thresholds.
  • Establish contracts and agreements with sites, vendors and collaborators; register the trial.

Engagement & Knowledge Translation

Stakeholder & Patient Engagement

Engage clinicians, community members and patients early. Define roles along a spectrum from consultation to empowerment, and compensate patient partners appropriately.

Communications & Knowledge Translation

Develop a communication strategy to disseminate trial insights to clinicians, policy‑makers and communities. Use open educational resources, webinars and clear, accessible materials.

Outputs & Administration

Protocol, Reporting & Archiving
  • Maintain versioned protocols and standard operating procedures.
  • Generate structured reports and exports (PDF, CSV) on demand.
  • Keep an audit trail and archive snapshots for long‑term access.
AI Assistance & Extensibility

The Navigator offers AI-driven feedback at every step and a modular architecture to incorporate additional domains (e.g., statistics, inclusivity, financing) as trialists’ needs evolve.

Pragmatic Trial Competency Wheel

Explore Competency Domains
  • Intent & Question: Center purpose around patient impact.
  • Trial Design: Ensure designs are pragmatic and ethical.
  • Implementation & Impact: Plan integration into routine care and measure real‑world effects.
  • Statistics: Outline analysis plans, including handling of missing data.
  • Ethics, Consent & Regulations: Respect consent processes and regulatory frameworks.
  • Trial Materials: Develop clear materials and training documents.
  • Partnerships & Support: Engage sponsors, sites and communities.
  • Indigenous Rights & Data Sovereignty: Honour Indigenous data governance.
  • Quality Control & Oversight: Implement risk‑based monitoring and quality assurance.
  • Data: Ensure integrity, interoperability and security.
  • Inclusion, Diversity, Equity & Accessibility: Promote equitable recruitment and interventions.
  • Financing & Resources: Plan budgets, secure funding and allocate resources.
